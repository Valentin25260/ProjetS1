André Léo (1824 – 1900)

She is a feminist activist who is a novelist. She published a text defending the equality of the sexes, which was at the origin of the first feminist wave. She will obtain a notoriety in the world after having published this text.

Olympe de Gouges (1748 – 1793)

She is known as the author of French feminism. It is her who will write the Declaration of the rights of the woman and the citizen. She was among the first women to demonstrate to claim the equality between men and women.

Madeleine Pelletier ( 1874 – 1939 )

Known for her political commitments, and for being the first female doctor to graduate in psychiatry, she psychiatry she will not cease to fight for the right to vote.

Malala Yousafzai

Born in 1997, she was already a feminist figure at the age of 24. She fights so that girls to go to school. She received the Nobel Peace Prize at the age of 17. She will also be victim of an assassination attempt by taking a bullet in the head but but she survived by a miracle.

Simone Veil ( 1927 – 2017 )

She is the first president of the European Parliament. She becomes an icon of feminism by succeeding in making adopt the Veil law, this law authorizes to the woman to abort.

Taylor Swift

Born on December 13, 1989, she is now a feminist figure thanks to her worldwide influence. She fights against the inequalities to the women by changing her style in her clips.

Hubertine Auclert (1848 – 1914)

Thanks to her relentless fight for women's rights, Hubertine Auclert is one of the figures of feminism. of feminism. She launched a newspaper called "La Citoyenne" where she defended the rights of women of women and the right to vote.

Julie Daubié (1824 -1874)

First woman to obtain the baccalaureate in 1861 but also the first woman to obtain a degree of letters at that time when women were not allowed. She wrote books on women's freedom and rights.
