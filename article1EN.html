Beginning of the feminist in France

The first French feminists appeared in France in the early 1860s and end in 1945. André Léo, whose real name was Victoire Léodile Béra, took advantage of the relative freedom promoted by the second Empire which is directed by Napoleon Bonaparte at that time, to publish works dedicated to the equality of the sexes. In 1866, she creates the "Association for the improvement of women's education" and in 1868, she publishes a text defending the equality of a text defending the equality of the sexes which is at the origin of the first French feminist group. She was not the only one to defend these ideas, there was also Julie Daubié who was the first woman to obtain the to obtain the baccalaureate in 1861.

Despite the beginning of the feminist movement that is starting to become more and more concrete there are some misunderstandings about what is important. One group prefers to emphasize one group prefers to emphasize the education of girls while the other wants civil equality above all. In 1878, takes place the International Congress of the right of the women which is centered on the improvement of the right in the current life (right of work, recognition of the domestic work, etc.). Very few people were interested in political rights, except for a minority of people who demanded equality between men and women.

The end of the nineteenth century is impacted by the beginning of groups that are created with the aim of having complete equality. The majority of the feminists agree to opt for a strategy on the long term. But this is not the case for all women. Some are more virulent like Hubertine Auclert who wishes the tax strike and to register the women on the electoral lists. electoral lists.

The advancement of feminism

On July 14, 1881 the first feminist demonstration organized by Hubertine Auclert took place, this demonstration with goal to stop the refusal to grant rights to the women. Following this demonstration the word feminism takes all its sense because until now the word was used for men where men where the virility stopped developing. From the 1890s onwards, more and more feminist feminist associations are created. Their goal is not the same for all the associations are each different. In 1891 there is a French federation created to regroup the feminist societies which will allow to make evolve the rights of the women and civil rights. Thanks to these movements new rights were accepted like the fact to be able to divorce.

Photo of women demonstrating for the right to vote

In 1909 the feminists created the French Union for Women's Suffrage. The Union evolved rapidly, from 6,000 to 12,000 members from 1912 to 1914. They doubled the number of members in only 2 years. Many newspapers were published at that time such as La Suffragiste by Madeleine Pelletier, the themes discussed were the right to vote, sexual harassment... The first world war slowed down the feminist movement because they privileged the efforts of the war. Moreover, women had to stay at home because of the men who had gone to fight. In 1930, the rise of unemployment sent women back to their homes. This period marked the end of the the first wave of feminism. in 1861.
